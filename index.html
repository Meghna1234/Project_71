The Writing Club
The Writing Club

The Writing Club is open to all 4th and 5th grade students who are interested in creative writing. Students in the club will participate in National Novel Writing Month in November and also help create the new school Literary Magazine. Club meetings will be most Mondays from 8:15am-9:00am in the Library.

Chess Club
Chess Club

Greenfield’s chess club is a chance for students to gather and play chess with friends before school. There is no formal instruction, and all skill levels are welcome. Chess Club meets on the third Friday of each month, from 8:30-9:00 in the cafeteria.

STEAM
STEAM

Students on the STEAM Team will meet after school and tackle science, technology, engineering, art, and math challenges! STEAM uses authentic situations and to build critical thinking and problem solving skills through application. Members must apply yearly to be a part of the team.

Campus Life

Library

The Greenfield Elementary Library provides services for both students and teachers. The library is open to students from 9:10 – 3:45. Students are scheduled to come to the library weekly for planned lessons and check out, but with teacher permission, they can visit the library throughout the day. Our mission statement The mission of the Greenfield Elementary School library program is to empower students to become life long readers and learners who are ethical users and creators of ideas and information.

  • THE STORY SQUAD

    The Chesterfield County Public Schools’ Office of Family and Community Engagement is excited to announce the CCPS Story Squad! The purpose of the Story Squad is to showcase and celebrate the hard work of our elementary students, their families and our teachers. Students can record goals they meet, books they have read, or awesome things that they have learned during the week. There will also be an opportunity for students to share how they have helped others and give shoutouts and recognition to people who have helped them!
